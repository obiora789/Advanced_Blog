OTHER CAREER ACHIEVEMENTS

As a graduate of the Federal University of Technology Owerri and a professional engineer, I have been working at the Nigerian Airspace Management Agency in Ikeja, Lagos, since 2013. Over the course of my career, I have had the privilege of working in two different roles: first as an air communications engineer and later as an air traffic controller.

During my time as an Air Communications Engineer, I was responsible for maintaining and repairing the communication systems used by pilots and air traffic controllers to communicate with one another. This was a crucial role, as clear communication is essential for ensuring safe and efficient air travel. Over the course of my four years in this position, I achieved several notable accomplishments.

First and foremost, I was able to develop a deep understanding of the communication systems used in air traffic control such as VHF and HF radios, radar systems, navigational aids, and other critical communication infrastructure, and was able to troubleshoot and repair these systems quickly and effectively when they experienced problems. This allowed me to minimise downtime and ensure that communication systems were always available when they were needed.

In addition, I was able to identify several opportunities for improvement in our communication systems. Specifically, I recognised that there was a need for more redundancy in our systems so that if one system failed, there would be backup systems in place to ensure that communication could continue. Over the course of several months, I was able to successfully implement the redundant systems implementation strategy that my coworkers and I had developed.

My contribution to the successful installation and commissioning of air communication equipment in several airports across the nation was made possible by this plan. This was an essential component of our service delivery since, in the absence of fully operational communication systems, air traffic controllers would be unable to efficiently interact with pilots operating in the area, raising safety issues. I helped increase the safety of aviation travel in Nigeria by making sure these systems were properly installed and operating as needed.

Finally, I was able to build strong relationships with my colleagues in air traffic control, which helped improve communication and collaboration between our two departments. This allowed us to work together more effectively, and to identify and address issues more quickly.

After four years as an Air Communications Engineer, I decided to transition to a new role as an Air Traffic Controller. In this role, I was responsible for managing air traffic and ensuring the safe and efficient movement of planes through Nigerian airspace. This was a challenging role, but one that I found incredibly rewarding. Over the course of my time as an air traffic controller, I achieved several significant accomplishments.

First, I was able to develop a deep understanding of the complex regulations and procedures that govern air traffic control. This allowed me to manage air traffic with confidence and competence, and to make quick and effective decisions when necessary.

Second, I was able to develop strong relationships with my colleagues in air traffic control, which helped to improve collaboration and communication within our department. This allowed us to work more effectively as a team, and to identify and address issues more quickly.

Finally, I was able to play a key role in the implementation of new technology in our air traffic control systems. Specifically, I worked closely with a team of engineers to implement a new radar system that allowed us to track planes more accurately and efficiently. This system was a significant improvement over our previous system and allowed us to manage air traffic with greater precision and safety.

In conclusion, my time at the Nigerian Airspace Management Agency has been incredibly rewarding and fulfilling. I am proud of the accomplishments I have achieved in both my roles as an Air Communications Engineer and as an Air Traffic Controller, and I look forward to continuing to make a positive impact in the years to come.
